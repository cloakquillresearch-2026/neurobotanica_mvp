Lesson 1.1: Why Most Cannabis Recommendations Are Guesswork

The THC Percentage Myth

Most dispensaries sell cannabis based on THC percentage alone. Customers walk in asking for "the strongest stuff," believing higher THC = better effects.

The problem? Clinical research shows this is completely wrong.

Two products with identical THC percentages can produce completely different effects based on:

  • Terpene profiles
  • Minor cannabinoid content (CBG, CBN, CBC)
  • The entourage effect
  • Individual endocannabinoid system variations

What Clinical Research Actually Tells Us

The NeuroBotanica database contains 505+ peer-reviewed studies on cannabis therapeutics. Here's what they consistently show:

  • Dose-response is non-linear: More THC doesn't always mean stronger effects
  • Whole-plant medicine outperforms isolates: The entourage effect is real
  • Terpenes modulate cannabinoid effects: Myrcene + THC = sedation; Limonene + THC = energy
  • Individual variation is huge: Genetics, tolerance, and ECS health all matter

Your Role as an Evidence-Based Budtender

Your job is to help customers find the right product for their specific needs, not sell them the highest THC percentage. This course will teach you how to:

  • Read cannabinoid and terpene profiles like a scientist
  • Match products to customer health goals
  • Explain complex science in accessible language
  • Make recommendations backed by clinical evidence
